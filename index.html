Homeschooling in Arizona

How to begin — clearly, and by the book.

Arizona is one of the most homeschool-friendly states in the country. Here is the path, with the legal essentials in plain language. We help every family choose and file the right way.

  1. 01

    Talk with us & plan

    We map the academic year, set up Acellus courses, and align school around training, travel, and competition.

  2. 02

    File your Affidavit of Intent

    For children ages 6–16, file a notarized Affidavit of Intent to Homeschool with your County School Superintendent within 30 days of starting, with a certified copy of the birth certificate.

  3. 03

    Build the full program

    Cover the five required subjects through Acellus, then layer in athletics, leadership, and mental-performance development.

  4. 04

    Begin & keep records

    Start instruction with automated progress, attendance, and transcripts — so eligibility and applications are always handled.

Quick reference

The Arizona essentials

Who files
Children ages 6–16. A.R.S. §15‑802
When
Within 30 days of beginning home instruction (or of the child's 6th birthday).
Where
Your County School Superintendent — Maricopa County for most of Greater Phoenix.
What to include
A notarized Affidavit of Intent plus a certified copy of the birth certificate or other reliable proof. A.R.S. §15‑828
Required subjects
Reading, grammar, mathematics, social studies, science.
One-time filing
File once unless homeschooling stops and restarts; notify the county within 30 days of stopping.
Choosing the path
The right enrollment route can differ by program — we help you confirm whether the homeschool affidavit or another pathway fits your family.

Requirements can change — confirm current details with your County School Superintendent and the Arizona Department of Education before filing. This page is general information, not legal advice.
